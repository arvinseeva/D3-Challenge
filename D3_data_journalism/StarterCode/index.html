Understanding Obesity

From this chart, Arkansas, West Virginia and Mississippi has the most obese populous. Families that are living in poverty and low-income are most likely to be obese. This is also more evident in the middle-aged group. We can conclude that finances are a driver in obesity. Low income or poverty leads to poor diet and subsequently obesity over long time.

Understanding Smoking

The state with the highest number of smokers are West Virginia and Kentucky. The evidence shows that poverty or low income leads to smoking. It is interesting to observe that the smokers in these states are also older with the median age around the 40s.
